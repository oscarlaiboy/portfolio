Urban Outfielders

Compete with others at a live Baseball game. Make predictions on the outcome of a players at bat and rack up points to win a prize!

  • Needfinding
  • Field Observations

  • Competitive Analysis

  • User Testing
  • Mockups

The Challenge

The challenge was to use technology to design a new social computing experience to increase social interaction.

Field Observations

In the beginning of the ideating phase I decided to go to a live baseball game one weekend. It was my first baseball game and I realized there were a lot of problems and interactions at the ballpark. I took note of different problems and did a quick interview with a few people on their time at the game.



Needfinding

After I analyzed all the data I've gotten from research and observations I created a list of user needs.


  • Wants to meet a new person

  • Friendly competition

  • More engagement in the game

  • Needs something fun to do during breaks
  • Wants to win a prize

  • Become an expert and be correct

  • Doesn't want to listen to people heckling

What's already been done...

I then did a quick competitive analysis between similar apps that already exists. A general rundown of the different attributes each app offers.



Interactive MVP

I created an interactive minimum viable product of what my app would be like. I used google sheets to create the mvp. Using different equations I created the 3 main pages to make this prototype work. A answer page, a selection page for the players, and a rankings page to see how well they did.



User Testing and Mockups

After I polished the mvp, I tested it in front of 100 students and staff. To create the setting I projected a live baseball game onto the big screen and then had a set of instructions for everyone to follow as if they were at a live game. After getting feedback on different aspects of the mvp I created mockups for the app.

Future Startup

Moving forward I could create a business model for this app and one day making it become real. Another idea going forward is this could become a live gambling app for people at the game. Over 50% of people from the class felt this would be more interactive if money was involved.
